Coupon Acceptance Analysis

Overview
This project explores what factors influence a driver's decision to accept or reject coupons delivered during various driving scenarios. The goal is to identify patterns in behavior using demographic, contextual, and behavioral data.

Key Findings

1. Overall Acceptance Behavior
A substantial portion of drivers accepted coupons, indicating that mobile coupon delivery can be effective. However, acceptance varies depending on context and user characteristics.

2. Bar Coupons: Behavior Matters Most
Drivers who frequently visit bars are much more likely to accept bar-related coupons.
Frequent bar-goers showed significantly higher acceptance rates than infrequent visitors.

3. Passenger Type Influences Decisions
Drivers traveling alone or with friends are more likely to accept coupons.
Drivers with children are less likely to accept.

4. Carryout Coupons: Convenience is Key
Acceptance is highest when drivers are alone or with a partner.

5. Combined Factors Improve Prediction
Drivers are more likely to accept coupons when:
- They frequently visit related venues
- They are not traveling with children
- They have flexible schedules

Conclusion
Coupon acceptance is influenced by behavior, context, and demographics. The most responsive customers are those whose priot behavior aligns with the offer.

Business Implications
- Target frequent users
- Deliver offers during flexible moments

Tools Used
Python (Pandas, Seaborn, Matplotlib)
