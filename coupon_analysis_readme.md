# 🚗 Coupon Acceptance Analysis

## 📌 Overview

This project explores what factors influence whether drivers accept or
reject coupons delivered during simulated driving scenarios. The goal is
to identify patterns in behavior using demographic, contextual, and
behavioral data.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 📊 Key Findings

### 🍺 Bar Coupons: Behavior Matters Most

Drivers who frequently visit bars are much more likely to accept
bar-related coupons.

### 👥 Passenger Type Influences Decisions

Drivers traveling alone or with a partner are more likely to accept
coupons, while those with children are less likely.

### 🌡️ Environmental Factors

Acceptance is higher during afternoons and evenings and when drivers are
heading home or have no urgent destination.

### 🥡 Carryout Coupons: Convenience is Key

Acceptance is highest when drivers are alone or with a partner and lower
when traveling with children or commuting.

### 🧩 Combined Factors

Drivers are more likely to accept coupons when: - They frequently visit
related venues\
- They are not traveling with children\
- They have flexible schedules

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 📈 Conclusion

Coupon acceptance is influenced by behavior, context, and demographics.
The most responsive customers are those whose habits align with the
offer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 💡 Business Implications

-   Target frequent users\
-   Deliver offers during flexible moments\
-   Avoid targeting constrained drivers

------------------------------------------------------------------------
